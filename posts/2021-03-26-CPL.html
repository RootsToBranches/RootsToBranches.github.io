Calgary Public Library has a veritable treasure trove of resources centered around family history and its storytelling. Christine Hayes over at CPL was kind enough to provide a compilation of links showcasing their offerings:

  • Here is a really touching story of reconnection, highlighting just what library and information resources can facilitate: https://calgarylibrary.ca/library-news/david-greer-library-story/

  • Newcomer's Stories Collection compiles first-generation immigrants and their tales of landing and living in Canada: https://library.biblioboard.com/anthology/1288c9ff-e5be-4da4-9beb-f0509620969b

  • Members of the Treaty 7 Nations and Calgary residents have shared their pandemic stories with Calgary Public, in collaboration with storyteller pro Richard Van Camp (to add a story just click in the Submit to Project): https://library.biblioboard.com/anthology/2f4a9d29-2d82-48c5-8d89-264d19643bd8

  • Digitized history of Calgary and Southern Alberta at the William and Harris Shared History Centre: https://calgarylibrary.ca/read-learn-and-explore/digital-library/williams-and-harris-shared-history-centre/

  • Access Ancestry Library Edition from home until June 30th here: https://calgarylibrary.ca/read-learn-and-explore/digital-library/?category=history-and-genealogy

  • The Virtual Family History coaching program helps those interested in researching and compiling their family histories with professional one-on-one consultations with researchers with genealogist volunteers from the Alberta Family History Society: https://calgarylibrary.ca/events-and-programs/programs/virtual-family-history-coaching/
